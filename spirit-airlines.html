Spirit Airlines

Advertising Brand Strategy

Spirit Airlines built its brand on low fares, but after a failed JetBlue merger, bankruptcy headlines, and media-fueled fear around flight safety, trust took a nosedive (no pun intended). With routes slashed and ticket sales slipping, Spirit isn't just battling competitors — it's battling perception.

To challenge this, Spirit needs to go all in on transparency.

Type

Advertising Campaign / Brand Strategy

Objective

Rebuild trust and reposition Spirit as the smartest way to fly on a budget

Approach

Own the low-cost model with radical transparency and honesty

The Challenge

People don't trust the brand, and they confuse "cheap" with "unsafe."

My first impression: everyone loves to hate Spirit. The objective is clear — rebuild trust and reposition Spirit as the smartest way to fly on a budget.

Who We're Speaking To

We're not speaking to luxury travelers. We're speaking to the ones who fly with purpose, not perks.

47%

18–34 year olds are more likely to fly Spirit than the general population. Side hustlers, gig workers, and creators who know how to stretch a dollar.

58%

of Spirit flyers are men. Less brand loyalty, more price sensitivity. They book what makes sense, not what sounds fancy.

48%

fall into the lower third of monthly income. Calculated spenders — not broke, just budget-savvy.

They're frequent flyers, not frequent complainers. They're destination-focused, deliberate, and decisive. They know that free snacks aren't really free, and they'd rather keep the $75.

What They're Saying

We talked to college students and others who fit Spirit's demo about how they see budget airlines. Here's what we heard:

"It ends up costing the same as a regular airline. You get tricked by the low price, then pay for everything else."

They feel misled — not by the price, but by the lack of transparency.

"I never trusted Spirit. It just feels sketchy."

They're skeptical. Even if they've never had a bad experience, the brand's reputation does the damage.

"I'd rather pay more upfront than worry about getting nickel-and-dimed."

They want control. Not more freebies. Just fewer surprises.

Meet the Target

JR

Josh Rivera

Age 24 · Fort Lauderdale, FL · Wedding Photographer

Josh is part creative, part entrepreneur, and he's building his business one flight at a time. He books gigs across the country, travels light, and edits on the go. His schedule is flexible, but his income isn't always steady. That means every trip has to make sense — financially and logistically.

Josh has flown Spirit before. He knows what he's getting, but he still hesitates. Not because of the service, but because of the uncertainty. Is he going to get hit with surprise fees? Is the plane safe? Is it worth it?

He doesn't need a drink cart or complimentary pretzels. He needs a brand that levels with him. Josh doesn't want upgrades. He wants clarity. And that's what builds trust.

The Truth: Spirit Isn't Cutting Corners. They're Cutting Nonsense.

The Work

Not free snacks. Not fake luxury. We're selling clarity. Control. And choice. Spirit isn't the airline you settle for — it's the airline you understand. Because when you know what you're paying for, you're not flying cheap. You're flying smart.
